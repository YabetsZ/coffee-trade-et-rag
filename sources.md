# Coffee trade regulation data sources for Amharic RAG

Use these sources to collect a small document corpus for the project.

Verified on: April 15, 2026.

## Primary sources

1. Ministry of Agriculture, Ethiopia
   - Website: https://www.moa.gov.et/
   - Useful for: coffee trade policies, extension notes, policy summaries
   - Verified: the site contains Amharic news and agricultural policy content.

2. Ethiopian Institute of Agricultural Research (EIAR)
   - Website: https://www.eiar.gov.et/
   - Useful for: coffee research reports, trade support notes, quality information
   - Note: if the homepage is not easy to scrape, look for PDF reports, publications, or search results.

3. Ethiopian Coffee and Tea Authority
   - Website: https://www.eca.gov.et/
   - Useful for: coffee quality, value chain, export, and policy materials
   - If the exact site changes, search for the official Coffee and Tea Authority website.

4. Agricultural Transformation Institute (ATI)
   - Website: https://www.ati.gov.et/
   - Useful for: technology adoption, value chain reports, farmer training content
   - Verified: the site has current agricultural news and extension-related content.

5. FAO Ethiopia
   - Website: https://www.fao.org/ethiopia/
   - Useful for: sustainable agriculture, market systems, and coffee-related materials
   - Verified: the site contains Ethiopia-specific publications and news pages.

## Secondary sources

6. Regional agriculture bureaus
   - Examples: Amhara, Oromia, Tigray, SNNPR regional websites
   - Useful for: local extension manuals and farmer training documents

7. University repositories
   - Search Ethiopian university thesis repositories
   - Useful for: coffee theses, surveys, and extension studies

8. Local news and media articles
   - Useful for: short Amharic explanations and coffee industry updates

## Recommended starter collection set

If you want a small but strong corpus, collect 5–10 documents from these types:

- Coffee trading proclamation
- Coffee buying and selling rules
- Coffee grading and quality rules
- Coffee export or licensing procedure
- Coffee market or value-chain summary
- Coffee history and culture

## Best format for the corpus

- Plain text (.txt) extracted from PDFs or web pages
- 1 topic per file
- 300–1,000 words per file is enough for a class project
- Keep the content in Amharic when possible

## Collection tips

- Prefer PDF manuals, extension leaflets, and web pages with clear paragraphs.
- Save plain text versions into data/corpus/.
- Keep each file focused on one coffee topic.
- Add a short source note at the top of each document if useful.
